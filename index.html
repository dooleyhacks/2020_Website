Participants have 36 hours to create anything that shows off their creativity and passion for development. You may choose any platform, programming language, or format to show your stuff. No worries! Prior to the event, we'll be sending out our Hacker guide, detailing the ways we think you can make the most out of your weekend. During the event, the DooleyHacks team will be available for questions. As always, feel free to reach out to us at hello@dooleyhacks.com if you have any other questions!

Anyone who is 18 or older (Yes, we check IDs!) and a currently enrolled college student with a valid student ID is eligible for our event.

Make sure to bring your photo ID, laptop, phone, chargers, toiletries, a change of clothes, and anything else you may want during the weekend! Make sure to bring a government-issued ID if you plan on checking out hardware.

We encourage everybody to get in teams of up to 4 Hackers, and we will help facilitate this with a team-forming event at the start of the Hackathon, as well as a slack channel for team-formation that all participants will be added to prior to the weekend. However, teams are not necessary, so feel free to work individually.

Currently, travel reimbursement is not something we are able to provide for our Hackers. However, this is contingent on response and sponsorship, so please let us know in your application if you'll need reimbursement, and we'll see what we can do!

As college students ourselves, we understand how busy things can get. You're welcome to come and go from the event as you please, but make sure to keep your registration badge on you at all times, without it you won't be re-admitted to the venue!
